# GL Assessment 11+ Landscape — 2026/27 Admissions Cycle

**Research compiled:** 17 April 2026
**Scope:** 11+ exams sat September 2026 for September 2027 Year 7 entry
**Purpose:** Grounding document for a premium GL-focused prep PWA (Path A). Four subjects only: English, Maths, Verbal Reasoning, Non-Verbal Reasoning.

---

## 1. GL Assessment 2026/27 Format — Summary Table

GL Assessment is the dominant test provider for UK selective state grammar schools, supplying papers or the underlying test bank to the majority of the c.163 English state grammars [Explore Learning; Atom Learning]. Papers are **paper-based, non-adaptive, multiple-choice**, with answers recorded on an OMR (Optical Mark Recognition) answer sheet. **GL does not negatively mark**, so pupils should attempt every question [Atom Learning].

| Subject | Typical duration | Typical question count | Format | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| English | 50 minutes | 49–56 | Multiple-choice | Single ~2-page comprehension text (~13 Qs) + SPAG/cloze sections (~4 each) [Atom Learning; Visuteach] |
| Maths | 50 minutes | ~50 | Multiple-choice | KS2 curriculum, weighted toward number [Atom Learning; Explore Learning] |
| Verbal Reasoning | 50–60 minutes | 60–80 | Multiple-choice | Drawn from bank of 21 question types [Eleven Plus Exams Ltd; Exam Happy] |
| Non-Verbal Reasoning | 50–60 minutes | ~80 (4 sections × 20) | Multiple-choice | Drawn from bank of ~12 NVR + 7 spatial types [Atom Learning; Exam Happy] |

**Total testing time:** approximately 3.5–4 hours including breaks, though most areas split the load across two sittings or combine subjects into two 50–60-minute multi-subject papers (Bucks, Kent, Bexley model) [Atom Learning].

**Two common packaging models used by admissions authorities:**

1. **Four discrete subject papers** (e.g. Kendrick) — one paper per subject.
2. **Two combined papers** (e.g. Buckinghamshire STT, Kent PESE, Bexley Selection Test, QE Boys) — e.g. "Paper 1: English + Maths; Paper 2: VR + NVR", each ~50–60 minutes.

Format changes 2024/25 → 2026/27: **No structural changes to the GL paper design itself.** The notable movement is at admissions-authority level — **Bexley has dropped GL for 2026 entry onwards and switched to Quest Assessments** [Atom Learning; Bexley Council; Prep4All]. Buckinghamshire **renewed its 5-year GL contract** covering 2026 entry onwards [TBGS/Prep4All]. Reading School and Chelmsford County use **FSCE** (Future Stories), not GL [Prep4All; CCHS]. Essex CSSE schools (Colchester Royal, CCHSG) use the **bespoke CSSE paper** (English + Maths, partly free-response) [CSSE; Atom Learning].

## 2. Per-Subject Question Type List (Definitive Authoring Spec)

### 2.1 English

Sources: Visuteach, Atom Learning, GL familiarisation materials.

- **Reading comprehension** (single ~2-page fiction or non-fiction passage; ~13 questions testing inference, deduction, vocabulary, author intent, language analysis, tone, literal retrieval).
- **Spelling** — identify the misspelt word in a sentence, or the sentence with no spelling error; typically 4–8 questions.
- **Punctuation** — identify the punctuation error (missing comma, misused apostrophe, speech marks, capitalisation); 4–8 questions.
- **Grammar** — identify word class (noun, verb, adjective, adverb, preposition, pronoun, conjunction); subject–verb agreement; tense consistency; 4–8 questions.
- **Cloze — word bank** — fill gaps in a passage choosing from a provided word list (each word used once).
- **Cloze — jumbled letters / missing letters** — reconstruct a word where letters are scrambled or missing.
- **Sentence completion / "complete the sentence"** — choose the word/phrase that makes the sentence grammatically correct or contextually best.
- **Vocabulary (synonyms / antonyms / meaning in context)** — choose word closest / opposite in meaning, or define a word as used in the passage.
- **Spot the mistake** — a sentence contains exactly one error; identify which underlined segment is wrong.

### 2.2 Maths

Sources: Atom Learning, Explore Learning, Sparkling Minds, KS2 National Curriculum.

- **Number and place value** (rounding, ordering, negative numbers, Roman numerals)
- **Four operations** (mental arithmetic, long multiplication, long division, order of operations / BIDMAS)
- **Fractions** (equivalent, simplification, + – × ÷, fraction of an amount, mixed numbers)
- **Decimals** (place value, × and ÷ by powers of 10, converting to/from fractions)
- **Percentages** (percentage of amount, percentage change, fraction/decimal/percent conversions)
- **Ratio and proportion** (sharing in a ratio, scaling recipes, direct proportion)
- **Algebra** (simple equations, forming expressions, function machines, sequences with nth term — introductory only)
- **Measurement** (length, mass, volume/capacity, time, money; metric units and conversions)
- **Perimeter, area and volume** (rectangles, compound shapes, triangles; cuboid volume)
- **Geometry — properties of shape** (angles in triangles/quads, parallel lines, reflective/rotational symmetry, 2D/3D shape properties, nets)
- **Geometry — position and direction** (coordinates in four quadrants, translations, reflections)
- **Statistics / data handling** (bar charts, line graphs, pie charts, pictograms, mean, mode, median, range, timetables)
- **Multi-step problem solving / word problems** (frequently fraction-of-amount, percentage, speed–distance–time)

### 2.3 Verbal Reasoning — the 21 Canonical GL Types

Source: Exam Happy (based on GL familiarisation + NFER heritage), cross-checked Eleven Plus Exams Ltd and Prep4All. Numbering is the industry-standard convention; GL does not publish official "type numbers" but assessment authors use this taxonomy consistently.

1. **Type 1 — Crack the letter code** (word ↔ letter code pairs, work out the missing code or word).
2. **Type 2 — Complete the letter series** (sequence of letter pairs; find the next pair).
3. **Type 3 — Complete the letter sentence** (insert two letters into analogy "AB is to CD as EF is to ??").
4. **Type 4 — Create a compound word** (one word from list A + one from list B → compound).
5. **Type 5 — Find the missing letter** (one letter completes several words simultaneously).
6. **Type 6 — Move a letter** (remove one letter from word 1, insert into word 2; both must remain valid words).
7. **Type 7 — Find the missing three-letter word** (a hidden 3-letter word completes a longer word inside a sentence).
8. **Type 8 — Create words in the same way** (spelling pattern transfer between word groups).
9. **Type 9 — Solve letter sums** (letters stand for numbers; solve the arithmetic).
10. **Type 10 — Continue the number series** (number sequence — identify the rule and next term).
11. **Type 11 — Find the hidden four-letter word** (a 4-letter word straddles two adjacent words in a sentence).
12. **Type 12 — Find words closest in meaning** (pick the synonym pair from two lists).
13. **Type 13 — Odd ones out / related words** (three words go together; identify the two that don't fit).
14. **Type 14 — Find the word link / complete the sentence** (select correct words from multiple banks to complete a sentence).
15. **Type 15 — Explore the facts / logic puzzle** (information grid — who did what / sat where).
16. **Type 16 — Find words opposite in meaning** (antonym pair from two lists).
17. **Type 17 — Find the number to complete the sum** (numeric equation with a missing operand).
18. **Type 18 — Find the missing number** (numeric analogy or sequence).
19. **Type 19 — Crack the number code** (word ↔ number code; decode unknown word).
20. **Type 20 — Complete the third pair in the same way** (word-based analogy with spelling/letter manipulation).
21. **Type 21 — Find the double meaning / most related** (a fifth word that fits with four given words on a specific shared meaning).

### 2.4 Non-Verbal Reasoning + Spatial Reasoning

Source: Exam Happy, Atom Learning, 11PlusGenie.

**Non-verbal (12 core types):**

1. Most unlike (odd one out from 5 shapes)
2. Most like — three figures (identify which of 5 shares features with the given three)
3. Most like — two figures (same task from only 2 source figures)
4. Series / sequence (4- or 5-square row with one missing)
5. Extended series (longer rows; two missing positions)
6. Triangle series (six-triangle sequence; find missing triangle)
7. Analogies (A is to B as C is to ?)
8. Matrices (3×3 or 2×2 grids with one cell blank)
9. Similar triangles (subdivided triangular matrices)
10. Star matrices (12-triangle star pattern, one missing)
11. Horizontal codes (3-letter codes classify shapes; find code for test shape)
12. Vertical codes (2-letter codes — identify shared attributes)

**Spatial reasoning (7 types, tested in Kent, Bucks and other spatial-inclusive papers):**

1. Hidden shapes (find a target shape hidden inside a composite)
2. Figure / paper folding (hole-punch + unfold prediction)
3. Cube nets (2D net → 3D cube matching)
4. Block counting (count blocks in a 3D stack including hidden ones)
5. Matching shape / rotation (identify the same shape after rotation)
6. Jigsaw (pick the piece that completes the shape)
7. Combining shapes (add/subtract marked shapes to form target)

## 4. GL Official Resources + SAS Scoring Explainer

### 4.1 GL-published familiarisation materials

From gl-assessment.co.uk / 11plus.gl-assessment.co.uk:

- Six free downloadable ZIP packs (PDF format) covering: Verbal Reasoning; Non-Verbal Reasoning; English; Maths; Non-Verbal Reasoning + Maths (combined); Verbal Skills.
- Collectively advertised as **"10 hours of free familiarisation materials."**
- GL also sells paid practice books at three difficulty tiers per subject — "three practice books containing three question papers and answer sheets each."
- Published disclaimer: paper structure and timings **vary by admissions authority** and parents should verify locally.
- Familiarisation papers are **not past papers** — GL does not release live test content; real test items are re-used across years and kept confidential.

### 4.2 Standardised Age Score (SAS) mechanics

Mathematical definition (universally applied, confirmed across GL Education support pages, Atom Learning, Cambridge Insight, BOFA, 11plustutorsinessex):

```
SAS = 100 + 15 × ((raw_score − mean) / standard_deviation)
```

Properties:

- **Mean = 100, Standard deviation = 15** (by design — GL applies this explicitly).
- Scale practical range: **60 to 170** (clipped at tails).
- **Age adjustment** is applied before standardisation: GL uses the candidate's age in **years and completed months** on test day. Younger children receive an effective upward adjustment; older children a downward one. This prevents pure age-in-year bias (a child born in August is not penalised vs a child born in September).
- **Normal distribution assumption:** ~68% of the population score between 85 and 115; ~95% score between 70 and 130.

Percentile landmarks (derived from normal distribution, confirmed by Atom Learning and elevenplusexampapers.co.uk):

| SAS | Approx percentile | Plain-English meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 100 | 50th | Exactly average for age |
| 111 | ~77th | Top quarter |
| 115 | ~84th | "Ready to sit the 11+" benchmark (Atom) |
| 121 | ~92nd | **Top 8% — Bucks STT qualifying score** |
| 125 | ~95th | Top 5% — typical superselective threshold |
| 130 | ~98th | Top 2–3% — top tier of superselective cohort |
| 140+ | ~99.6th | Top 0.4% — SAS ceiling typically ~142 |

### 4.3 Typical superselective cut-offs (2024/25 published data, best proxy for 2026/27)

- **Buckinghamshire STT:** Published qualifying score **SAS 121** (weighted: 50% VR, 25% Maths, 25% NVR) — buckinghamshire.gov.uk.
- **Kent Test:** Aggregate **≥332** across 3 components with **no single component <106**. In SAS terms approximately equivalent to averaging ~111 across English, Maths and Reasoning — lower than Bucks because Kent is county-wide, not superselective.
- **QE Boys:** Combined score **220/280** across English + Maths (a raw cumulative, not pure SAS). Actual SAS cut-off typically quoted by tutors as **≥135** in both subjects [Exam Papers Plus].
- **Henrietta Barnett Round 1:** Top 300 invited to Round 2 — effective SAS ≥130 average required [Achieve Learning].
- **Latymer:** Top 700 after Maths+VR marked; final offer cut-off typically ≥130 combined [Atom Learning].
- **Kendrick, Tiffin Girls:** Cut-offs routinely ≥130 [Atom Learning; Marie Redmond].

**Honest caveat:** Individual school cut-offs fluctuate year-to-year by 2–5 SAS points depending on cohort strength and are rarely published openly. Tutoring-site aggregations (exampapersplus, Atom Learning, MMEreivse) are the best proxy and should be treated as guidance, not absolute.

## 5. Known Changes / Upcoming Changes for 2026/27

1. **Bexley leaves GL.** For September 2026 entry onwards, the Bexley Selection Test is provided by **Quest Assessments**, not GL. Format remains two mixed-subject multiple-choice papers of ~50 min each. Practice-paper libraries for Bexley pre-2025 remain GL-style but are no longer perfect matches. [bexley.gov.uk; Prep4All]
2. **Buckinghamshire renewed GL contract.** TBGS awarded a new 5-year contract to GL Education Group Ltd starting with the September 2025 test (for 2026 entry). Format unchanged. [thebucksgrammarschools.org]
3. **No structural changes to the GL paper design.** Underlying item bank, subject coverage, SAS methodology, question-type taxonomy all stable since 2019 refresh.
4. **Chelmsford County & Reading School continue with FSCE** — not relevant to GL coverage but worth noting that parents who target CCHS alongside GL grammars need dual prep.
5. **Sutton SET format stable** for 2026/27 — two-paper multiple-choice sift plus bespoke stage 2 [sutton.gov.uk]. Shares format DNA with GL but is school-authored.
6. **Watch-points for 2026/27:** some forums note GL has modernised its **digital trial platform** (GL Ready/Exact), but live 11+ tests remain paper-based for 2026/27. No migration to on-screen testing for grammar admissions is scheduled.
