# 11+ Mathematics: Detailed Research

## Topic Areas

### Number and Arithmetic
- Place value up to 10,000,000, negative numbers
- Four operations: long multiplication (4-digit × 2-digit), long division (4-digit ÷ 2-digit)
- BODMAS/BIDMAS order of operations
- Mental arithmetic: times tables to 12×12 (rapid recall essential)
- Factors, multiples, primes (to 100), HCF, LCM
- Square numbers (to 15²=225), cube numbers (1,8,27,64,125), square roots
- Estimation and rounding
- Negative numbers in context

### Fractions
- Equivalence, simplification, ordering/comparing
- Addition/subtraction with unlike denominators
- Multiplication (by whole numbers and fractions)
- Division by whole numbers
- Mixed numbers ↔ improper fractions
- Fractions of amounts (e.g., 3/5 of 240)

### Decimals
- Place value to 3 decimal places
- All four operations with decimals
- Multiplying/dividing by 10, 100, 1000
- Converting fractions ↔ decimals

### Percentages
- Converting fractions ↔ decimals ↔ percentages
- Finding percentages of amounts
- Percentage increase/decrease
- Reverse percentages (stretch)

### Ratio and Proportion
- Ratio notation and simplification
- Dividing quantities in a given ratio
- Proportion problems (scaling recipes)
- Unitary method

### Algebra (Basic)
- Simple formulae
- Linear number sequences
- Solving equations: 3x + 7 = 22
- Expressing word problems algebraically
- Two unknowns (stretch)

### Geometry
- **2D shapes**: triangles (equilateral, isosceles, scalene, right-angled), quadrilaterals (all types), polygons, circles
- **3D shapes**: cubes, cuboids, prisms, cylinders, pyramids, cones, spheres; nets, faces/edges/vertices
- **Angles**: measuring/drawing with protractor; on a straight line (180°), at a point (360°), in a triangle (180°); vertically opposite; missing angle problems
- **Symmetry**: lines and rotational
- **Position**: coordinates in all four quadrants; translation, reflection, rotation

### Measurement
- Metric conversions (km/m/cm/mm, kg/g, l/ml)
- Imperial ↔ metric approximations
- Perimeter (rectangles, compound shapes)
- Area (rectangles, triangles, parallelograms, compound shapes)
- Volume (cubes, cuboids)
- Time (12/24-hour, intervals, timetables, speed-distance-time)
- Money (all operations, best buy, profit/loss)

### Data Handling
- Tables, two-way tables, timetables
- Bar charts, pictograms, line graphs, pie charts
- Mean, median, mode, range
- Drawing conclusions, comparing datasets

### Number Sequences
- Linear (constant add/subtract)
- Non-linear (square numbers, triangular numbers, Fibonacci-style)
- Finding nth term rule (stretch)
- Spatial/visual patterns linked to number sequences

### Problem Solving
- Multi-step word problems
- Logic and deduction puzzles
- Working backwards
- Trial and improvement
- Systematic listing

## Question Formats

### Multiple Choice (GL)
- 5 options (A-E), ~1 minute per question
- Distractor answers match common errors (e.g., cost appears as option when change is asked for)
- No marks for working — only final answer counts

### Short Answer (CEM, ISEB)
- Write numerical answer in a box
- CEM: maths embedded in language-rich contexts (reading comprehension also tested)
- ISEB: working expected, partial marks for correct method

## Difficulty Tiers

### Tier 1 — Foundation (Year 4-5)
Single operation, straightforward numbers, obvious method.
- "What is 347 + 258?"
- "A rectangle: length 7cm, width 4cm. Area?"
- "Next in sequence: 3, 7, 11, 15, ___"

### Tier 2 — Intermediate (Year 5-6)
Two+ steps, choosing right operation, may involve conversion.
- Train journey with delay — calculate normal duration
- Bag of sweets: 3/8 red, 25% blue — how many neither?
- Multi-step money problems

### Tier 3 — Challenging (Stretch/Above Year 6)
Multiple interconnected steps, abstract reasoning, combining topics.
- "Two numbers: sum 56, difference 18. Find the product."
- Cuboid filled with smaller cubes — how many fit?
- Reverse percentages, compound changes

## Stretch Content (Differentiates Top Scorers)
- Solving equations with unknowns
- Complex ratio problems
- HCF/LCM using prime factorisation
- Reverse/compound percentages
- Area of parallelograms/trapeziums, surface area
- Calculating mean from frequency tables, finding missing values
- Speed/distance/time with unit conversion
- Coordinates in all four quadrants
- Non-linear sequences
- Systematic enumeration, "show that" problems

## Common Mistakes
1. **Times tables errors** (7×8, 6×7, 8×9, 12×7)
2. **Carrying/borrowing errors** in column arithmetic
3. **Misreading the question** — finding cost when asked for change
4. **Unit conversion** — 1m=100cm errors; treating time as decimal (1h30m ≠ 1.3h)
5. **Adding fractions wrong** — adding numerators AND denominators (1/3 + 1/4 = 2/7)
6. **Percentage conversion** — thinking 5% = 0.5 instead of 0.05
7. **Ordering decimals wrong** — thinking 0.19 > 0.2
8. **Confusing area and perimeter** (extremely common)
9. **Forgetting to halve** for triangle area
10. **Only completing part** of a multi-step problem
11. **Not checking reasonableness** (negative age, 500 children in class of 30)
12. **Time management** — too long on hard questions, missing easy ones later
