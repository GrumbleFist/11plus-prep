# 11+ English: Detailed Research

## Question Types

### 1. Comprehension
The centrepiece of 11+ English. Tests a hierarchy of reading skills:
- **Literal retrieval**: Find explicit information in the text
- **Inference**: Read between the lines — feelings, motives not stated directly
- **Deduction**: Combine multiple clues to draw conclusions
- **Language analysis**: Why did the author choose that word/technique?
- **Summary/synthesis**: Condense information in own words
- **Evaluation**: Form and justify opinions about the text

**What makes a strong answer (PEE/PEEL structure)**:
- **P**oint — state the answer
- **E**vidence — direct quotation from text (embedded, not copied chunks)
- **E**xplanation — why that evidence supports the point
- **L**ink — back to the question

Analytical vocabulary: "suggests", "implies", "conveys", "demonstrates" (not just "shows" or "says")

### 2. Synonyms and Antonyms
- Easy: common words (happy/sad, big/small)
- Hard: Latinate/literary words (benevolent/malevolent, loquacious/taciturn, ostentatious/modest)

### 3. Cloze Passages
Passage with blanks — select or supply the correct word. Tests vocabulary, grammar, and contextual understanding simultaneously.

### 4. Sentence Completion
Select word/phrase that best completes a sentence grammatically and semantically.

### 5. Spelling
- Identify incorrectly spelled word from options
- Select correct spelling from variants
- Correct errors in a sentence
- **Commonly tested**: separate, necessary, accommodation, occurred, definitely, embarrassed, independent, parliament, environment, immediately, occasionally, privilege, recommend, rhythm, thorough, mischievous

### 6. Grammar
- **Parts of speech**: nouns (common, proper, abstract, collective), adjectives, verbs (including modal), adverbs, pronouns (personal, possessive, relative), prepositions, conjunctions (coordinating + subordinating), determiners
- **Tenses**: simple, progressive/continuous, perfect — all past/present/future
- **Sentence types**: simple, compound, complex; statements, questions, commands, exclamations
- **Clauses**: main and subordinate, relative clauses
- **Active/passive voice**
- **Direct/reported speech** (pronoun and tense shifts)
- **Subject-verb agreement** with complex subjects
- **Formal vs informal register**

### 7. Punctuation
- Full stops, question marks, exclamation marks
- Commas: lists, fronted adverbials, clause separation, parenthetical
- Apostrophes: contraction vs possession (including irregular plurals)
- Inverted commas/speech marks (punctuation inside, new speaker = new line)
- Colons and semicolons
- Hyphens in compound adjectives
- Brackets, dashes, commas for parenthesis

### 8. Creative/Extended Writing
Not in GL. Sometimes in CEM (limited). Major component in ISEB.
Assessed on: imagination, vocabulary range, sentence variety, SPaG, structure, reader engagement.

## Vocabulary Expectations
- Significantly exceeds National Curriculum for age 10-11
- Need reading age 2-3 years ahead of chronological age
- Understanding of **word roots, prefixes, suffixes** (Latin/Greek: bene-, mal-, aqua-, -ology, pre-, anti-)
- No single official word list, but resources suggest 500-1500+ targeted words
- Key resource: Susan Daughtrey's "The 1100 Words You Need to Know for the 11+"
- High-frequency 11+ words: abundant, acknowledge, adversary, apprehensive, arid, audacious, benevolent, candid, cautious, commence, conceal, conspicuous, contempt, defiant, diligent, diminish, elaborate, endeavour, exquisite, formidable, futile, genuine, gratitude, immense, impeccable, indignant, jubilant, luminous, magnificent, notorious, obscure, peculiar, perilous, plausible, prominent, prudent, reluctant, serene, solitary, substantial, tedious, tranquil, unanimous, vague, vigilant, wretched

## Difficulty Progression

| Factor | Easier (Year 3-4) | Harder (Year 5-6+) |
|---|---|---|
| Vocabulary | Common, everyday | Abstract, Latinate, literary |
| Sentences | Simple and compound | Complex, multiple subordinate clauses |
| Inference | Shallow, feelings nearly stated | Deep, combining multiple clues, subtext, irony |
| Passage genre | Modern, age-appropriate fiction | Pre-20th century prose, poetry, dense non-fiction |
| Answers | MC with obvious distractors | MC with close options, or free-response |
| Grammar | Identify a noun or verb | Distinguish subordinating conjunction from relative pronoun |

## Common Mistakes (10-year-olds)
1. Copying chunks instead of own words
2. Not using text evidence
3. Superficial inference
4. Misreading the question (what vs why)
5. Not reading whole passage carefully
6. Ignoring command words (explain ≠ identify)
7. Confusing similar words (affect/effect, complement/compliment, stationary/stationery)
8. Common misspellings (separate, definitely, occurred, accommodation, receive)
9. Tense inconsistency within writing
10. Subject-verb agreement with complex subjects
11. Misusing apostrophes (it's vs its, plural vs possessive)
12. Comma splices
13. Confusing there/their/they're, your/you're
14. Starting every sentence with "I" or "The"
15. Telling not showing ("He was angry" vs "His jaw tightened")
16. Time management — spending too long on one question
