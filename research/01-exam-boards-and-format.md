# 11+ Exam Boards, Format, and Structure

## History and Purpose

The 11+ was introduced under the **Butler Education Act of 1944** to sort children at age 11 into grammar schools (academic), technical schools, or secondary moderns. Most of the UK abolished it in the 1960s-70s, but **163 grammar schools remain in England** and **67 in Northern Ireland**.

**Pass rates**: National average ~11% (1 in 9). Varies hugely: Buckinghamshire ~37%, superselective schools 3-5%.

## Exam Boards

### GL Assessment (Dominant Provider)
- Near-monopoly since CEM's 2022 withdrawal from paper-based testing
- **Four separate papers**: English (50 min), Maths (50 min), Verbal Reasoning (60 min), Non-Verbal Reasoning (60 min)
- Primarily **multiple choice** (5 options, A-E)
- Scores are **age-standardised** (Standard Age Score, mean 100, SD 15)
- Target SAS of **120+** for grammar school entry
- **Regions**: Kent, Buckinghamshire, Lincolnshire, Lancashire, Cumbria, North Yorkshire, Plymouth, Bournemouth, Wiltshire, parts of Devon, Essex, Birmingham (post-CEM switch), Warwickshire, Trafford, Wirral, Bexley, Redbridge, Slough, Gloucestershire, Wolverhampton

### CEM (Durham University) — Largely Withdrawn
- Designed to be "tutor-proof" — no published past papers, format changes each year
- **Two mixed papers** combining subjects (English+VR, Maths+NVR)
- Withdrew from paper-based testing in 2022, affecting ~50 schools
- Successor: **Cambridge Select Insight** (computer-based), limited uptake
- Most former CEM schools switched to GL

### ISEB (Independent Schools)
- Used by private/independent schools (Eton, Harrow, Westminster, etc.)
- **Four papers**: English (25 min), Maths (50 min), VR (36 min), NVR (32 min)
- Delivered **online**, some adaptive elements
- Created by GL Assessment

### CSSE (Essex Consortium)
- 10 grammar schools in Essex
- **Two papers** (60 min each): English (comprehension + writing) and Maths (written answers, not MC)
- Notable for including a **writing component** and requiring **shown working**

### FSCE (Future Stories Community Enterprise)
- Tests **KS2 National Curriculum only** — no NVR
- Used by: Chelmsford County High, Colyton Grammar, Heckmondwike Grammar, North Halifax Grammar, Reading School, Blue Coat School Liverpool, Crossley Heath

### Northern Ireland — SEAG
- Single common transfer test from 2023 (merged former AQE and GL systems)
- Two papers (60 min each) on consecutive Saturdays in November
- Tests English and Mathematics equally, mixed MC and open-response
- Cost: £50

## Typical Timeline (England)

| When | What |
|------|------|
| Year 4 / Early Year 5 | Preparation begins |
| March-May (Year 5) | Registration opens |
| May-August (Year 5) | Registration deadlines |
| Early September (Year 6) | Exams sat |
| October (Year 6) | Results released |
| 31 October | Common application deadline |
| 1 March (Year 6) | National Offer Day |

## Recent Trends
- CEM's exit made GL near-monopoly provider
- VAT on private school fees (2025-26) expected to increase competition
- Long-term trend toward digital/online testing
- Greater emphasis on contextual vocabulary over rote memorisation
